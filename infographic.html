👩‍🏫 Teachers

Primary Concern:
"Will this actually save me time or just add to my workload?"
Sales Insight:
Demo time-saving features first, not engagement bells and whistles. Lead with "Do Magic" lesson creation, not student response tools.
What They Need to Hear:
Preparation time savings, easy integration with existing workflows, immediate classroom value.

👥 Administration

Primary Concern:
"How do we measure success and justify the investment?"
Sales Insight:
Always include measurement strategy in proposals. Connect features to strategic plan goals, not just engagement metrics.
What They Need to Hear:
Clear ROI data, pilot success stories, alignment with district strategic priorities.

💻 IT & Data Teams

Primary Concern:
"Will this create security headaches or compliance issues?"
Sales Insight:
Lead with security credentials and provide technical documentation upfront. Address compliance before features.
What They Need to Hear:
FERPA compliance, data encryption, SSO integration, minimal infrastructure requirements.

🎯 Technology Coaches

Primary Concern:
"How do I support teachers through another technology change?"
Sales Insight:
Position as a coaching tool, not just a classroom tool. Emphasize built-in PD resources and peer collaboration features.
What They Need to Hear:
Training resources, teacher support systems, quick wins for early adopters.

The Strategic Sales Advantage

Understanding stakeholder psychology transforms generic pitches into strategic conversations

Key Sales Takeaway:

Successful EdTech sales requires different conversations with different stakeholders. The IT director needs to hear about security first. The curriculum coach wants to know about standards alignment. The teacher cares about prep time. One-size-fits-all pitches fail because they don't speak to anyone's specific concerns.
