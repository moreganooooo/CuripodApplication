Question 1

What is the hardest thing you've accomplished?

Being the first outbound sales hire at Treering to surpass $1M in sourced revenue while simultaneously rebuilding trust in an industry notorious for exploiting schools. Administrators were skeptical, and rightfully so. Traditional yearbook companies had locked them into expensive contracts with upfront costs, leftover inventory, and administrative nightmares. Schools were choosing between their students' memories and their budgets.

I didn't just need to sell a product; I needed to dismantle a broken system and prove that EdTech could serve educators rather than burden them. Through patient, personalized outreach and strategic territory management, I helped countless schools cut per-book costs in half, launch yearbook programs for the first time in their history, and eliminate financial risk. The skepticism was fierce, but the transformation was profound. I exceeded my first-year target by 17% and the kind, respectful messaging I developed that year became our team-wide template for years.

The hardest part wasn't the sales metrics; it was earning trust in a space where trust had been repeatedly broken. Every conversation required proving that technology could make educators' lives easier, not harder. That experience taught me that exceptional sales in EdTech isn't about convincing. It's about listening, understanding, and delivering solutions that genuinely serve.

Question 2

How would you position Curipod to a teacher, Technology Director, and Curriculum Director?

To a Teacher:

Imagine sparking authentic curiosity across every subject, whether students are exploring complex scientific concepts, analyzing historical events, or preparing for state assessments. Curipod transforms your classroom into a space where every voice matters and every student feels heard. The AI-powered feedback meets learners exactly where they are, whether they're crafting persuasive essays, solving mathematical problems, or diving into literature discussions.

Your students will engage with content they care about while building the critical thinking skills that matter far beyond any test. You're not just covering curriculum — you're cultivating the kind of genuine learning that reminds you why teaching chose you.

To a Technology Director:

Curipod delivers what every technology investment promises, but rarely achieves: adoption that spreads organically because of its immediate value. Even educators who typically resist EdTech ask for access once they experience our platform's intuitive design and measurable impact.

You'll enjoy seamless integration, robust security, and the kind of authentic teacher buy-in that transforms pilot programs into district-wide success stories. It's not another tool to manage; it's the solution that makes your existing investments more effective.

To a Curriculum Director:

When schools see 22% to 46% achievement gains from targeted writing practice, that's systemic transformation. Curipod aligns with modern standards while addressing the real challenge: engaging every student in rigorous learning that builds critical thinking, communication, and collaboration skills.

We implement research-backed pedagogy that closes achievement gaps while preparing students for futures that demand both academic excellence and authentic human connection. The data speaks for itself, as do the teachers who've watched struggling students turn into confident communicators.

Question 3

Why are you excited to join the Curipod team?

Your team embodies everything I've been searching for in my next role. You're real humans solving real problems with innovative technology and genuine empathy. I love the way you've positioned yourselves as facilitators of human connection, and I genuinely agree that the best EdTech should enhance human interaction rather than replace it.

I'm particularly drawn to your commitment to sparking curiosity and making questioning "something you do every day, because it is fun and energizing." That philosophy resonates deeply with my approach to sales and relationship-building, as evidenced by my love of A/B testing and personalization (even at scale!), my creation of a Content Committee from the ground up, and my initiative in carving out new outreach processes.

Having spent nearly a decade building bridges between technical solutions and the educators who need them, I'm thrilled at the opportunity to represent a platform that equally serves teachers and students. Curipod is a thoughtful response to the challenges facing modern education, and I would love to be part of that.
