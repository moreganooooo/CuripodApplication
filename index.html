The Email That Changed Everything

When I joined Treering, there were no scripts or tools — just a Gmail account and the green light to figure it out.

I relied on what I knew: how to write in a way that feels human. Instead of using generic templates, I built outreach from scratch — warm, respectful, and paced to reflect the real demands on educators’ time. Those messages worked so well they became the foundation of our first outbound templates and helped me exceed quota by 17% in my first year.

It worked because it wasn’t about selling. It was about listening.

The PTA Council That Changed Everything

Most teams were trained to move on when a school said “district handles publishing.” I decided to push — gently.

I started a pilot to engage PTA Councils directly. One Georgia council invited me to present to every PTA leader in the district. That conversation opened the door to district-level approvals, and eventually, statewide promotion by Georgia PTA.

The impact was massive — and helped reshape how we approached sales across the org.

Building a Role from Scratch — for Someone Else

One of my direct reports wanted to grow beyond SDR work. I saw the perfect match between her strengths and a critical need in our CRM strategy.

I led a project that uncovered $3M+ in dormant pipeline, then used that success to create a new role focused on research and ops. I built her dashboards, training, KPIs, and support tools so she could hit the ground running.

She thrived — and so did our pipeline. It’s one of my favorite examples of strategic thinking + people leadership coming together.

The Onboarding Lightbulb

It often took new SDRs nearly a school year to fully “click” with our outbound process. So I turned my internal brain-map into something everyone could use: a custom onboarding website.

It walked through workflows, tools, browser setup, Salesforce tags, and more — complete with diagrams and team resources. It was so effective it became part of formal onboarding and dramatically reduced ramp time.

My favorite part? Helping new teammates go from overwhelmed to confident.

Segmented for Humans

I led a yearlong email series that became our team’s gold standard: segmented by school level and role, tailored for every audience.

I built, wrote, and QA’d every message myself — not just to “optimize,” but to make sure each contact felt seen and understood. That empathy translated into long-term trust and open doors — even with the hardest-to-reach stakeholders.

It wasn’t just a campaign. It was connection, at scale.
